## Cybercrime Type: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

---

## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick victims into approving fraudulent payment requests or sharing sensitive financial credentials such as UPI PINs, OTPs, or bank account details. It is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India, targeting millions of smartphone users every year.

---

## Case Study: The Fake Customer Care Scam

**Scenario (Realistic Fictional Case)**

Rohan, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, recently opened a bank account linked to his UPI app. One afternoon, he received a call from someone claiming to be a customer care executive from his bank, informing him that his UPI account would be blocked within 24 hours unless he completed a "KYC verification."

The caller sounded professional and knew Rohan's name and partial account number — details easily obtained from data breaches or social media. He instructed Rohan to install a screen-sharing app called AnyDesk to "complete the verification process remotely." Once installed, the attacker gained full visibility of Rohan's screen.

The attacker then sent Rohan a "collect request" on his UPI app — which is actually a request to receive money from Rohan, not send it to him. He told Rohan to "approve the request to verify your account." Rohan, believing this was part of the verification, entered his UPI PIN and approved the request.

Within seconds, Rs. 15,000 was transferred out of his account. The call disconnected immediately. When Rohan called back, the number was switched off.

---

## How It Happened – Step by Step

1. **Reconnaissance** – Attacker obtained Rohan's name and partial bank details from a data breach or public source.
2. **Impersonation** – Called pretending to be a bank customer care executive, creating a sense of urgency.
3. **Remote Access** – Convinced Rohan to install AnyDesk, giving the attacker screen visibility.
4. **Deception** – Sent a UPI collect request and falsely described it as a "verification step."
5. **Execution** – Rohan entered his PIN thinking he was verifying his account; money was transferred out.
6. **Disappearance** – Attacker disconnected and the number became unreachable.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- First-year college students unfamiliar with banking processes
- New UPI users who recently opened accounts
- Elderly individuals with limited digital literacy
- Anyone who has recently posted about a financial transaction on social media

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (often between Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 1,00,000)
- Psychological distress and loss of trust in digital banking
- Difficulty in recovering funds (UPI transactions are often irreversible)
- Identity theft risk if personal documents were also shared
