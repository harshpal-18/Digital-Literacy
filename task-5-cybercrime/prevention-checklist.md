## 🔒 General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** – Create passwords with a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never use your name, date of birth, or "123456" as a password. Use a different password for each account.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** – Turn on 2FA on all important accounts including email, social media, and banking apps. This adds a second layer of security even if your password is stolen.

3. **Never click on unknown links** – Do not click on links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email from unknown senders. These may redirect you to fake websites designed to steal your credentials.

4. **Keep your apps and phone updated** – Software updates often include security patches. Always update your banking apps, UPI apps, and phone OS when prompted.

5. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions** – Public Wi-Fi networks are unsecured and can be monitored by attackers. Never log in to your bank or UPI app on a public network.

6. **Do not install apps from unknown sources** – Only download apps from the official Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Never install APK files sent via WhatsApp or links.

---

## 💰 Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone** – No bank, UPI platform, government body, or customer care executive will ever ask for your UPI PIN or OTP. If someone asks, it is a scam — hang up immediately.

8. **Understand the difference between Send and Collect requests** – In UPI, a "Collect Request" means money will leave YOUR account. You do not need to enter your PIN to RECEIVE money. If anyone asks you to enter your PIN to receive money, it is fraud.

9. **Verify before scanning any QR code** – Always check the name of the recipient displayed after scanning a QR code before entering your PIN and approving the transaction.

10. **Never install screen-sharing apps (AnyDesk, TeamViewer) at a caller's request** – Legitimate bank or UPI customer care will never ask you to install such apps. These give attackers full visibility of your screen, including your banking apps.

---

## 🌐 Social Media & Identity Safety

11. **Do not share personal documents online** – Never send photos of your Aadhaar card, PAN card, or bank passbook over WhatsApp or email unless you are 100% certain of the recipient's identity.

12. **Regularly review app permissions** – Check which apps on your phone have access to your camera, microphone, contacts, and SMS. Revoke permissions that seem unnecessary.

## 🚨 If You Are a Victim – Report Immediately

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | https://cybercrime.gov.in |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (available 24x7) |
| **Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Call your bank immediately to freeze transactions |
| **Local Police** | File an FIR at your nearest police station |

> **Act within the first hour** – The faster you report, the higher the chance of recovering your money.
