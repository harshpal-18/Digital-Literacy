## 💻 Task 3 – Coding and Collaboration Platforms

For Task 3, I worked with two categories of platforms: a coding practice platform and a cloud collaboration tool.

---

### 🧠 Part A – Coding Practice (HackerRank)

For Part A, I created an account on HackerRank and completed the **"Solve Me First"** beginner challenge. This introductory problem required reading two integers and printing their sum — a simple but effective way to get comfortable with the platform's interface and submission system.  

I also explored the available skill tracks and noticed that HackerRank offers structured learning paths in Python, SQL, and Data Structures, which I plan to follow over the coming semesters.

---

### 📊 Part B – Google Workspace Collaboration

For Part B, I created a Google Form titled **"Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz"** with five questions. The quiz includes multiple choice questions on topics such as password safety and phishing, and a short answer question asking students to describe one safe browsing habit.  

The form is linked to a Google Sheet that automatically collects responses. These tools are extremely useful for academic collaboration — Google Forms can be used for feedback collection, quizzes, and event registrations, while Google Sheets provides real-time data organization.  

Both tools are part of Google Workspace, which is free for students and accessible from any browser.
