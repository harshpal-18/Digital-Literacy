# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 4**
**Harsh Pal | Reg No.: 25BCY10039 | VIT Bhopal University**

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep profiles professional** – Use a decent profile photo and a clear bio on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub. Employers and internship coordinators check these.

2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or parents saw this?" If not, don't post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** – Control who can see your personal posts. Keep your academic and professional profiles public, and personal accounts private.

4. **Give credit for shared content** – Always tag or mention the original creator when sharing someone else's work, images, or ideas.

5. **Report harmful content** – If you see cyberbullying, hate speech, or fake accounts, use the platform's Report feature. Don't just scroll past it.

6. **Keep your digital footprint clean** – Regularly review your old posts and remove anything that may appear unprofessional or offensive.

7. **Use verified information only** – Before sharing any news or message, verify it from a trusted source such as a reputable news outlet or official government website.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Never share OTPs, passwords, or PINs** – No bank, company, or government official will ever ask for this. Anyone who does is attempting fraud.

2. **Don't post negative remarks about your college or faculty publicly** – Address academic concerns through proper channels like email or the student grievance cell.

3. **Don't accept requests from strangers** – Do not accept friend, follow, or connection requests from people you cannot verify, especially on personal accounts.

4. **Never share your real-time location** – Avoid posting your live location, daily schedule, or home address on any public platform.

5. **Don't spread unverified information** – Never forward messages, images, or videos without checking if they are true. Misinformation spreads rapidly on social media.

6. **Don't engage with or respond to trolls** – Responding to hateful or provocative comments escalates conflict. Block or report instead.

7. **Never post during moments of anger** – Emotional posts are often regretted later and can have long-term consequences on your reputation.

---

## 📌 Remember
> Anything posted online is permanent. Even deleted content can be screenshotted and shared.
> Your digital reputation is part of your professional identity — build it wisely.
